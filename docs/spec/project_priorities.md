# Specification: Project Priorities & Workflow

This document outlines the strategic prioritization and operational workflow for the Exosystem development.

## 1. Strategic Priorities

Our immediate roadmap is prioritized as follows:

1.  **Scenario Pipeline Expansion**: Continuously developing the "Saga" through detailed scenarios that demonstrate the real-world application of our technologies.
2.  **Telemetry-Linked Verification**: Implementing real-time telemetry (via the Conscia/ExoTalk engines) to verify that the automated interactions within a scenario are executing correctly and predictably.
3.  **Comprehensive Automated Testing**: Leveraging the modularized plumbing (e.g., `exoauth`) to build a fully automated environment where agents can execute and validate complex multi-node workflows.

## 2. Operational Methodology

We follow a scenario-driven development cycle:

*   **Scenario First**: Development begins with the creation of a new Scenario (markdown) and Screenplay.
*   **Feature Gap Identification**: As we move from scenario to scenario, we identify missing technologies or features required to highlight specific use cases (e.g., identity management, scientific publishing).
*   **Just-in-Time Branching**: New "branches of activity" are created specifically to implement the technology necessitated by the current scenario.
*   **Demonstration as Validation**: A feature is considered "fully implemented" when it can be successfully demonstrated and verified within the context of its scenario's automated production pipeline.

## 3. Narrative-Driven Implementation

The final arc of our development—the "Saga"—culminates in a mission that demonstrates the life-saving potential of the Application Triad. 

In this narrative, **Jurgen**, a retired medical doctor in Berlin, is desperately trying to reach his daughter, **Isabella**, in East Jerusalem. He has uncovered critical medical research that Isabella needs to deliver to a doctor in a half-damaged hospital in Beirut, Lebanon. 

The traditional scientific publishing world has failed them; the paper contains "negative results" deemed unpublishable by legacy journals. Only by Jurgen convincing his research student to publish their work openly on **RepubLet** is the information liberated. Isabella, utilizing the interoperable power of the Exosystem, is able to see the research, secure the necessary permissions (granted only to her), and blindly deliver the payload to her anonymous contact in Beirut.

The scenarios we implement now, and those we add in the future, all build toward this climax. Isabella’s success is only possible because of the seamless interoperability between ExoTalk's family of independent, decentralized technologies—where an Exonomist, an Exocrat, and a RepubLetist can collaborate across borders and blockades to complete a mission that legacy systems simply cannot support.
